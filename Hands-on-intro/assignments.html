A Practical Approach to Updating Assignments

Updating assignments for an AI-enabled learning environment does not require abandoning existing course goals. Instead, it involves making small, intentional changes that shift emphasis from easily generated products to visible thinking and decision-making.

Step 1: Identify What the Assignment Is Really Assessing

Start by clarifying the core learning outcome. Ask yourself:

  • What should students understand, practice, or be able to explain after completing this?
  • Which parts of the task demonstrate reasoning rather than formatting or fluency?

If the outcome is primarily conceptual understanding, focus assessment on explanation, justification, and application rather than polished prose.

Step 2: Test the Assignment with AI

Before revising, try the assignment prompt in a generative AI tool. Observe what the model does well and where it struggles.

  • Does the AI produce a plausible but generic response?
  • Does it miss course-specific context or recent material?
  • Does it struggle with ambiguity or open-ended judgment?

These observations reveal which parts of the assignment need strengthening.

Step 3: Add One Layer of Process

Instead of redesigning everything, add a single requirement that makes student thinking visible.

  • A brief planning note or outline
  • An explanation of how sources were selected
  • A short reflection on key decisions or challenges
Example: “In 200–300 words, explain how you approached this task and why you structured your response the way you did.”

Step 4: Ground the Task in Course Context

AI systems perform best on generic prompts. They struggle more when tasks require local knowledge or specific classroom experiences.

  • Reference in-class discussions or activities
  • Require application to a shared dataset or case study
  • Ask students to connect concepts to their own project or research question

Step 5: Clarify Acceptable AI Use

Ambiguity about AI use increases misuse. Clear expectations support integrity.

  • Specify whether AI may be used for brainstorming, drafting, or revision
  • Require disclosure when AI tools are used
  • Explain how AI use will (or will not) affect grading
Key idea: Small changes—added context, reflection, or iteration—often do more to support learning than sweeping redesigns.

Conceptual Wrap-Up

Effective assignment updates align assessment with thinking rather than output. This approach supports academic integrity while preserving the goals of the original task.

Show Expanded Explanation

Generative AI excels at producing fluent text on familiar topics. Assignments that emphasize process, judgment, and context reveal learning in ways that remain meaningful even as tools improve.

Over time, these incremental changes build a more resilient assessment ecosystem.

Practical Guidance for Teaching and Academic Work

Start with one assignment rather than an entire course.

Share examples of acceptable AI-supported work with students.

Treat assignment updates as iterative—observe, revise, and refine over time.
