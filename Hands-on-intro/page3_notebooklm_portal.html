NotebookLM and Retrieval Augmented Generation

Now that you've seen the limits of a model’s context window, this activity shifts toward solving that problem through Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). NotebookLM is designed to ingest large documents, break them into chunks, and retrieve only the pieces relevant to your question. This grounding makes it dramatically more accurate with long, complex source materials.

Even more importantly for teaching, NotebookLM can now generate multimodal studio assets—presentations, outlines, audio summaries, and study guides—directly from your documents. In this activity you’ll upload the same 304-page Energy & AI PDF and create usable teaching materials from it.

Unlike Copilot or Gemini, NotebookLM uses only the uploaded documents when answering a question. This creates predictable, grounded responses and enables the creation of interactive learning features such as mind maps, video explanations, audio summaries, and structured study guides.

Step 1 — Uploading documents to Notebook LM

Login to Notebook LM with a Google account.

Create a new Notebook and add the Energy doc as the single source. You can always add more later, Notebook LM allows up to 50 individual sources for each Notebook.

Step 2 — Ask Questions in the Notebook Chat Window

Just like in Copilot, you type questions into the central chat window. But the results differ: NotebookLM only references the uploaded sources. Try asking:

“What are the primary arguments made in the first three sections?”

Notice how NotebookLM responds with citations and references drawn entirely from your sources. No external knowledge is blended in unless you uploaded it.

Key Difference: ChatGPT and Copilot blend general model knowledge with the document. NotebookLM does not. This makes it ideal for academic readings and student-facing assignments where grounding matters.

Step 3 — Generate a Mind Map

In the Studio panel, select “Generate Mind Map.” NotebookLM will create a visual branch structure of the document’s key ideas, sections, and relationships. This is especially effective for students who benefit from spatial or conceptual mapping.

You can  interact with the map by clicking nodes to reveal deeper connections or related excerpts. This turns passive reading into exploratory learning. When you click a node it passes that back to the chat window for deeper explanation.   

Step 4 — Generate a Video Explanation

Use the “Generate Video Summary” feature. NotebookLM will produce a short narrated video covering main themes, arguments, and evidence. This provides a different modality for students who learn more effectively through audiovisual content.

Many instructors use this to provide pre-class briefings or flipped-classroom preparation.

Step 5 — Create an Audio Overview

Use the “Audio Overview” tool. NotebookLM creates a spoken, conversational walkthrough of the document’s main points. Students can listen while commuting, exercising, or reviewing.

Step 6 — Create a Sharable Study Guide

Select “Generate Study Guide.” NotebookLM will produce a structured set of questions, explanations, summaries, and section breakdowns—based entirely on the uploaded documents.

You can share this Notebook with students so they explore the materials, ask their own questions, and interact with the assets you generated.

NotebookLM maintains academic integrity by grounding everything in the source material— no hallucinations based on outside information.

Conceptual Wrap-Up

NotebookLM serves as a focused learning portal built around the documents you choose. Students explore ideas in multiple modalities—text, mind maps, audio, and video—without risking drift into unrelated or incorrect general-information territory. This makes NotebookLM ideal for literature reviews, dense readings, technical texts, and structured course materials.

NotebookLM’s strict grounding in uploaded sources means it avoids a major failure mode of general LLMs: blending correct information from a document with plausible but inaccurate world knowledge. This is especially important in academic contexts where alignment with assigned readings matters.

The multimodal Studio tools—mind maps, video summaries, structured outlines, audio guides—sit on top of this retrieval architecture. Because each output is based entirely on your source documents, students can rely on the accuracy of the materials and see direct citations back to the original text.

By treating NotebookLM as a shareable learning environment, you shift from asking students to digest dense texts on their own to giving them a guided exploratory space where they can examine ideas visually, verbally, and interactively.
