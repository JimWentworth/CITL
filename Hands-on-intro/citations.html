Using AI Responsibly: Academic Integrity & Citation for Students

Generative AI tools can support learning when used thoughtfully. Academic integrity is not about avoiding AI entirely—it’s about using tools transparently, responsibly, and in ways that align with your course expectations.

Start with Your Instructor’s Guidance

  • Read your syllabus and assignment instructions carefully
  • Note what kinds of AI use are allowed, limited, or prohibited
  • Ask questions early if expectations are unclear

When in doubt, assume that you are responsible for explaining and justifying your work.

Key idea: Integrity issues usually come from unclear expectations or hidden use, not from asking questions or being transparent.

What Counts as Responsible AI Use?

In many courses, AI may be used to:

  • Brainstorm ideas or questions
  • Clarify concepts you don’t understand
  • Get feedback on organization or clarity
  • Revise wording or tone

AI should not replace:

  • Your analysis, reasoning, or interpretation
  • Required reading or research
  • Original problem-solving or reflection

How Integrity Issues Happen (and How to Avoid Them)

  • Submitting AI-generated work as your own: write in your own words and explain your thinking.
  • Using AI for restricted tasks: check assignment rules before using tools.
  • Copying AI output without review: verify facts and revise carefully.

When and How to Cite AI Use

If your instructor allows AI use, you may be asked to acknowledge it. This is about transparency—not punishment.

Simple disclosure example:
“I used an AI tool to brainstorm initial ideas and revise wording. All analysis, evidence selection, and final decisions are my own.”

Follow your instructor’s guidance on where to include disclosures (footnote, appendix, reflection, or assignment statement).

AI Is Not a Source—Cite Real Sources

AI tools may summarize or suggest ideas, but they are not scholarly sources.

  • Cite readings, articles, datasets, or media directly
  • Verify any facts or references suggested by AI
  • Never submit AI-generated citations without checking them

Use AI as a Reviewer, Not a Shortcut

Review my draft for clarity and organization. Point out unclear reasoning or places where I should explain my thinking more fully. Do not rewrite the content for me.

Conceptual Wrap-Up

Academic integrity in the age of AI is about responsibility, transparency, and judgment. You remain accountable for understanding, accuracy, and ethical use.

Show Expanded Explanation

Generative AI produces plausible language, not guaranteed truth. Using it responsibly means checking, revising, and making your own decisions.

Transparency protects you: when you clearly explain how tools were used, you reduce misunderstandings and build trust.

Practical Guidance for Students

When unsure, ask your instructor before submitting work.

Keep brief notes on how you used AI for major assignments.

Treat AI output as a starting point—not a final answer.
