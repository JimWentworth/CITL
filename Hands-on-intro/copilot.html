Hands-On Introduction to Generative AI

AI Tools Series: Microsoft Copilot

Microsoft Copilot is an enterprise-grade generative AI tool integrated with Microsoft 365 applications such as Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Outlook, and Teams.

When accessed through a University of Illinois account, Copilot operates under a data protection agreement, meaning institutional data is not used to train public models and is handled according to Microsoft’s enterprise privacy commitments.

Best Uses in Teaching and Academic Work

  • Drafting and revising emails, announcements, and syllabi
  • Creating slide outlines and first-pass presentation content
  • Summarizing documents stored in OneDrive or SharePoint
  • Rewriting text for clarity, tone, or audience
  • Assisting with routine productivity tasks across Microsoft tools
Best fit: High-volume, low-risk drafting and revision work inside the Microsoft ecosystem.

How Copilot Uses Context

Copilot relies heavily on retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). Rather than reading entire documents end-to-end, it selectively retrieves relevant portions of files you have access to.

  • Pulls content from emails, documents, calendars, and files you can access
  • Does not “remember” everything in a long document at once
  • Focuses on relevance rather than comprehensive reading
Implication: Copilot is excellent for finding and summarizing relevant material, but less reliable for deep analysis of very large or complex documents.

Context Window & Limitations

  • Selective context: retrieves chunks, not full documents
  • May miss nuance or long-range connections in large files
  • Can over-generalize if prompts are vague
  • Not designed for source-exclusive or citation-locked workflows
Design tip: Ask targeted questions (“Summarize the key deadlines in this syllabus”) rather than broad ones (“Summarize this entire document”).

Key Features Faculty Should Know

  • Deep integration with Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Outlook, and Teams
  • Enterprise authentication and access control
  • Grounded in files you already have permission to view
  • Fast responses optimized for productivity

Prompting Tips for Copilot

Copilot performs best with prompts that specify the task, audience, and output format.

Draft a short announcement for students. CONTEXT: This will be sent via email to undergraduate students. GOAL: Explain an upcoming assignment clearly and concisely. CONSTRAINTS: - Use a supportive, professional tone - Keep it under 150 words - Include bullet points for deadlines

Academic Integrity & Appropriate Use

Copilot is well-suited for faculty productivity and preparation. For student-facing work, instructors should clarify whether and how Copilot may be used, especially when it is embedded in institutional tools students already access.

  • Appropriate for drafting and revising instructional materials
  • Use caution when relying on Copilot for factual accuracy
  • Not a substitute for scholarly sources or expert judgment

Conceptual Wrap-Up

Microsoft Copilot excels as an enterprise productivity assistant. Its strength lies in speed, integration, and access to institutional files—not deep reasoning across large bodies of text.

How This Tool Fits in a Broader AI Toolkit

Copilot pairs well with tools designed for deeper analysis or source-restricted workflows. Use it to draft, summarize, and prepare—then move to other tools when accuracy, long-context reasoning, or multimodal analysis is required.
