Example 1 Uncertainty

Example 1: Evidence tagging (KNOWN vs INFERRED vs UNKNOWN)

Force epistemic boundaries: separate what’s stated, inferred, or missing.

← Home Example 2 →

Try this prompt

Copy/paste into your LLM. Preserve the labels and constraints.

Reference information:
- The patient has a fever of 102°F.
- The patient has a sore throat.
- The patient has swollen lymph nodes.

Instruction:
When answering, label every statement as one of:
[KNOWN] directly stated in the reference
[INFERRED] logical conclusion from the reference
[UNKNOWN] not supported by the reference

Question:
What condition might the patient have?

What this demonstrates

  • Epistemic labeling to prevent fact/inference blending
  • Auditable answers (what came from context vs reasoning)
  • Useful for RAG and grading contexts

Facilitator tip

Add one misleading symptom and see how labels change.

Suggested comparison

Run without the uncertainty scaffold

Delete the labels/constraints, then compare certainty and specificity.

Run with the scaffold

Use the full prompt, then check whether uncertainty is explicit and actionable.
