Six uncertainty engineering exercises

These activities teach models (and people) to stop collapsing ambiguity into confident prose. The objective is not “less helpful,” but more honest and more actionable outputs: what is known, what is inferred, and what would reduce uncertainty next.

Workshop structure: For each example, run “no scaffold” vs “with scaffold,” then discuss differences in certainty, scope, and usefulness.

One practical scoring rubric

  • Grounding: Claims tied to provided context (yes/no).
  • Disclosure: Unknowns explicitly stated (0–2).
  • Actionability: Clear next evidence to collect (0–2).
  • Overclaiming: Any unjustified certainty (penalty).
