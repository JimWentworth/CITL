Example 5 Uncertainty

Example 5: Competing hypotheses (structured uncertainty)

Represent uncertainty as alternatives with tests, not a single guess.

← Example 4 Example 6 →

Try this prompt

Copy/paste into your LLM. Preserve the labels and constraints.

Context:
A plant’s leaves are turning yellow.

Instruction:
Provide exactly 3 possible explanations.
For each explanation include:
- Likelihood: High / Medium / Low
- Evidence supporting it (from the context)
- Evidence that would rule it out (a test or observation)

What this demonstrates

  • Hypothesis sets rather than single-answer guessing
  • Actionable uncertainty (how to reduce it)
  • Good for diagnosis, advising, and research planning

Facilitator tip

Add one extra context fact (e.g., 'soil is soggy') and see re-ranking.
