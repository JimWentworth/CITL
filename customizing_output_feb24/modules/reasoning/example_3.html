Example 3: Representation engineering (change the output structure)

Show that forcing a representation can improve clarity and reasoning quality.

← Example 2 Example 4 →

Try this prompt

Copy/paste into your LLM. Preserve the step labels and constraints.

Topic: causes of World War I.

Produce THREE representations of the same explanation:
A) Bullet list (max 6 bullets)
B) Causal chain: Cause → intermediate cause → effect (4 links)
C) Table with columns: Factor | Mechanism | Evidence you’d look for

Then answer:
Which representation is most useful for teaching beginners, and why (2 sentences)?

What this demonstrates

  • Format shapes reasoning and audience suitability
  • Multiple representations expose gaps and overlaps
  • Teaching design becomes explicit

Facilitator tip

Swap topic to something in your discipline and compare which structure ‘reveals’ more.

Suggested comparison

Single-shot run

Ask once without structure; compare completeness and coherence.

Structured run

Use the scaffold; check whether reasoning is more stable and auditable.
