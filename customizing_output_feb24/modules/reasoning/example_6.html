Example 6: Evaluation engineering (rank answers using a rubric)

Train the model to judge quality explicitly, not just generate content.

← Example 5 Home →

Try this prompt

Copy/paste into your LLM. Preserve the step labels and constraints.

You are evaluating two student explanations of recursion.

Rubric (0–2 each; total /8):
A) Correctness
B) Clarity
C) Example quality
D) Common misconceptions addressed

Student A:
[PASTE ANSWER A HERE]

Student B:
[PASTE ANSWER B HERE]

Task:
1) Score A (A–D) and total
2) Score B (A–D) and total
3) Choose the better answer and give 2 reasons
4) Provide one concrete improvement for the weaker answer

What this demonstrates

  • Explicit evaluation criteria reduce arbitrary judgments
  • Comparative grading is often easier than absolute grading
  • Rubric engineering is a core faculty workflow

Facilitator tip

Swap the rubric to a department rubric and see if the model follows it faithfully.

Suggested comparison

Single-shot run

Ask once without structure; compare completeness and coherence.

Structured run

Use the scaffold; check whether reasoning is more stable and auditable.
