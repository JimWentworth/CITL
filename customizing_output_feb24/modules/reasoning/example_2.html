Example 2: Forced decomposition (prevent big jumps)

Improve reliability by splitting a complex task into ordered sub-tasks with constraints.

← Example 1 Example 3 →

Try this prompt

Copy/paste into your LLM. Preserve the step labels and constraints.

You are designing a lesson plan on photosynthesis.

Do these steps in order. Do not skip steps.
1) Write 3 learning objectives (Bloom verbs required).
2) List 4 common misconceptions.
3) Create 2 activities that directly address misconceptions.
4) Create 1 formative assessment aligned to objectives.

Constraints:
- Each step must be labeled (1–4).
- No step may exceed 80 words.

What this demonstrates

  • Decomposition improves completeness and alignment
  • Word limits reduce rambling and force prioritization
  • Step labels make outputs easier to audit

Facilitator tip

Remove step 2 (misconceptions) and see whether activities become less targeted.

Suggested comparison

Single-shot run

Ask once without structure; compare completeness and coherence.

Structured run

Use the scaffold; check whether reasoning is more stable and auditable.
