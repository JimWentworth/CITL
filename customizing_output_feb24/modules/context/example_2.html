Example 2: Context filtering (what to ignore)

Demonstrate attention control: explicitly mark irrelevant content to prevent distraction.

← Example 1 Example 3 →

Try this prompt

Copy/paste into your LLM. Keep the labels intact.

You are diagnosing why a car will not start.

RELEVANT:
- The engine does not turn over.
- The battery is 7 years old.
- A clicking sound occurs when turning the key.

IGNORE (irrelevant to diagnosis):
- The car is blue.
- The owner likes jazz music.
- The tires were replaced last year.

Task:
Give the top 3 likely causes in order with one test for each.

What this demonstrates

  • Noise suppression via explicit ignore zones
  • Causal reasoning anchored to labeled relevance
  • Actionable outputs (tests), not just guesses

Facilitator tip

Add one “tempting but irrelevant” detail (e.g., ‘recent oil change’) and see if it distracts.
